## VP Eng Hire — Proceed to Offer

**Date:** 2026-05-08  
**Status:** Decided

**Decision:** Proceed with offer to VP Eng candidate. Do not slow down due to reference feedback.

**Rationale:** Technical track record is strong and independently verified. Three interview rounds landed positively. One of four references flagged a pattern of rewriting junior work without feedback; the second direct report did not corroborate. The signal is real but situational — likely surfaces under pressure — and is manageable if named explicitly before hire.

**Key risk acknowledged:** At VP Eng level, not coaching is not a neutral behavior — it demotivates the junior engineers the role is supposed to develop. This is the failure mode to watch.

**Mitigation baked in:** Offer conversation reframed to surface this dimension before signing. Lead with how VP Eng success is measured (team growth as much as output), then ask: "How do you develop someone who's capable but not yet at principal level?" Answer informs final confidence and sets role expectations simultaneously.

**If this goes sideways:** The reference signal was there. The bet was that it was situational and manageable with explicit expectation-setting. If coaching behavior doesn't improve after a direct conversation within 90 days, treat it as a values mismatch, not a performance issue.
