# Requirements

## Structural

- The Foundations/DX split must be accompanied by an explicit intake and priority arbitration mechanism for Foundations work — scope clarity alone does not resolve the bottleneck
- The Platform team composition (who goes to Foundations vs. DX) must be decided deliberately, not incidentally — it materially affects DX's viability and Maya's credibility
- Darius's role in the new structure must be explicit in the proposal before it is finalized

## People

- The Maya conversation must happen before the proposal is final — her preference on management vs. technical depth is a design dependency, not a briefing detail
- The Darius and Lina conversations must be framed as co-design, not announcements — they should have input before the structure is locked
- The two Growth tech lead candidates must accept their roles privately before any announcement is made
- A contingency plan must exist for each tech lead promotion (what happens if one struggles in the first 90 days)
- The EM added under Lina must be clearly subordinate to her in structure and public framing — the risk of perceived undermining is high given her tenure

## Comms

- Comms sequencing must eliminate meaningful information gaps between leads — hours matter in a 30-person org
- ICs must hear about the reorg from their direct lead, not from the VP or all-org communications first
