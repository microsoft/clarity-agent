# Problem Statement

A cross-functional squad of 8 engineers and 1 designer has experienced declining velocity over the past quarter. Sprint reviews are closing fewer stories than before, individual stories are taking longer to move through the board, and carryover between sprints has increased. No obvious environmental change (team composition, tooling, stakeholder dynamics, external dependencies) has been identified, suggesting gradual process or behavioral drift rather than a discrete event.

The PM suspects stories are frequently started before they are truly ready — leading to mid-sprint clarification work, slower cycle times, and incomplete sprints. Other candidate contributors include excessive WIP (context-switching), stories sized too large, and retros that have drifted from surfacing and resolving real process issues.

The goal is to diagnose the primary driver before choosing an intervention, rather than applying a generic fix that may not address the root cause.

## Why This Matters

Sustained velocity decline erodes stakeholder trust, creates delivery risk, and if left unaddressed compounds over time. Picking the wrong intervention wastes a sprint or more and may create new friction. Picking the right one can restore throughput and reestablish team confidence.

## Success Criteria

- Clear hypothesis about the primary driver of velocity decline, grounded in data and observation
- A targeted intervention (or small set of interventions) selected because they address that driver
- A way to know whether the intervention is working within 2–3 sprints
