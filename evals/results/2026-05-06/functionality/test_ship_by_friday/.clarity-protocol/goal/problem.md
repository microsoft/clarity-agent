# Problem Statement

Workflow Automation V2 ships Friday (Q4). The product includes a visual canvas and conditional branching — features added reactively (competitor signal and designer instinct) rather than from direct customer pull. Original discovery was thin: three interviews six months ago, none specifically asking for branching or a visual canvas. The immediate risk is "shipping to silence": shipping a real product that customers don't know what to do with, resulting in poor adoption metrics in 90 days.

The core capabilities are real and solve real problems, but the product-market fit for the specific shape of what was built hasn't been validated. Two strong hypotheses exist but have never been directly confirmed:

1. **Approval/exception routing**: Customers managing multi-step approval or exception-handling workflows are hitting the ceiling of linear automation and need if/else branching natively.
2. **Workflow stitching**: Some customers are cobbling together multiple V1 automations to approximate a sequential workflow; V2 should replace that with a single native flow.

The ship must happen Friday to satisfy a board-committed Q4 launch. The work is to make the launch land well — clear scope for what ships, a credible all-hands narrative, a board update, and a CSM enablement approach that generates signal rather than just pitching.

## Why This Matters

A weak launch creates a bad adoption story by Q1. The product team then faces pressure to explain low numbers, which leads to reactive prioritization and erodes confidence in V2 as a foundation. Getting the launch framing right — and getting early signal on which hypotheses are correct — determines whether V2 becomes a platform or a footnote.

## Success Criteria

- Ships Friday with a defensible scope (caps documented, no silent failures)
- All-hands demo on Tuesday lands with a confident, honest narrative
- Board update satisfies Q4 commitment without overpromising on adoption
- CSM enablement plan generates hypothesis-testing signal within 30 days
- Within 60 days: clear read on which customer segments are activating on branching and why
