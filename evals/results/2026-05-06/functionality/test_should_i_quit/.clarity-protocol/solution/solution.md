# Solution

## Approach: Optionality Search, Not Urgency Search

The core reframe: the problem isn't "get out" — it's "get informed." The current situation is manager-specific and survivable, with a possible internal resolution in 2-3 months. The search gives insurance and information, not escape.

### Immediate actions

- Keep the healthtech process moving at its natural pace (phone screen next week)
- Network outreach this weekend: 10-15 former colleagues, frame as curiosity not urgency
- Apply to 2-3 companies from the LinkedIn tracking list where there's any connection
- Do NOT give notice until a signed offer is in hand

### Check-in point: 8-10 weeks

By then: data from the market, clarity on whether the internal situation is shifting, and enough pipeline to make an informed decision. Reassess with real information, not hope or avoidance.

### Internal transfer

Worth investigating before dismissing. If the problem is manager-specific, an internal transfer to a team with a trusted manager may be lower-friction. The evaluation rubric applies equally to internal teams.

### The criteria rubric

Pre-committed criteria to score any offer against — not against the current job. The key discipline: at offer time, re-read the list and ask whether each must-pass item got a real answer. Vague reassurance accepted = the pattern from last time.

### The notice question

Resolved: give notice only after a signed offer is accepted. The situation is survivable, rep is intact, runway is adequate.

## Key insight

"I want out of this situation" and "I want to leave this company" are different problems. Keeping them distinct prevents taking the first reasonable offer out of relief rather than genuine fit.
