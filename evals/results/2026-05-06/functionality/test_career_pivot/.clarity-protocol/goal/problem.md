# Problem Statement

Career transition into data science from a marketing manager role at a B2B SaaS company. The decision to transition has been made — the question is which entry path makes sense: part-time bootcamp or structured self-study. (Full-time bootcamp ruled out.)

## Context

- Currently employed; income continuity is non-negotiable (mortgage, two young kids, spouse works but can't absorb salary gap)
- Full-time bootcamp: ruled out — cannot absorb 3 months of no income
- Technical starting point: ML Fundamentals (Coursera certificate, completed), basic Python, some pandas — not a complete beginner; CAN sustain structured learning when there are clear deliverables and a bounded timeline
- Has started-but-not-finished unstructured Coursera paths before; needs accountability scaffolding to sustain effort
- Marketing background at B2B SaaS (4 years): domain expertise in attribution modeling, CAC/LTV, funnel analytics — a genuine competitive advantage for growth/marketing analytics roles, not a limitation
- Internal transfer not available: current company has no analytics function worth targeting
- Target timeline: 12–18 months to a first data science or analytics role
- Realistic hours/week: 10–12 (two young kids; higher estimates would be optimistic)
- Budget ceiling: ~$9k for a program; $15k+ is out of range
- Bootcamp appeal: structure, external accountability, cohort, credential — career services not a priority

## Why This Matters

Getting this path choice wrong means: (a) spending $9k on a bootcamp that's too time-intensive for a 10-12 hr/week schedule, (b) paying for structure they don't actually need, or (c) doing self-study without enough accountability and stalling out again.

## Success Criteria

A clear path recommendation with realistic expectations — which option fits these constraints, what the actual time/cost commitment looks like, and what the most important risks are for each path.
